Mechatronics Engineering

01.Robo War

  1. The challenge is to create a robot (manually controlled / autonomous) whose sole purpose is to push, throw, flip, drag, or otherwise move your opponent out of the arena within a stipulated time. This event aims to test your Robot against another in a field of combat where brute strength and cat-like reflexes hold the key to success. Who can participate Team must comprise of 1-4.
  2. The competition will be played on a knock-out basis.
  3. The bot will be inspected for safety before the event begins. If found to be unsafe for the participants/spectators, it will be discarded
  4. The organizers reserve the right to change any or all of the rules as they deem fit. Changes in rules, if any, will be announced on the time of event.
  5. Violation of any of the rules will result in immediate disqualification.
  6. The maximum time limit for the clash of combatants inside the arena is 10 minutes.(4 minutes round1-2minute break - 4minute round 2)
  7. A bot will be declared immobile (out) if it cannot exhibit linear motion of at least one inch within 30 seconds. A bot with one side of its drive train disabled will not be counted out if it can demonstrate some degree of controlled movement.
  8. It includes both offence and defense.
  9. Readymade kits and development boards are allowed.
  10. A Bot should be wireless, and advised to have two frequencies to operate for wireless bots.
  11. Teams that are not ready when called for battle will be considered to have declared a walkover, and will receive no points.
  12. The bot cannot be split into two sub-units. And the battery should be in the robot.
  13. Damaging the arena will lead to immediate disqualification
  14. In all cases, the judges' decision will be final and binding.

02.ROBO SOCCER

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  1. The robot should fit inside a box of 30cm*30cm*30cm.
  2. Robot should not exceed 6 kg.
  3. The robot can be only wireless. Participants should have two band widths for the wireless controls.
  4. The robot should be self-powered with supply not exceeding 12V (on board power supply).
  5. Power supply (only DC) should be on board. Participants cannot draw power from outside source.
  6. Only one participant should control the robot no player can be exchanged in the match.
  7. Team can have maximum number of three members.
  8. A robot can push or hit the ball. It cannot have any kick mechanism or grab the ball.
  9. Human interference (e.g. touching the robot) during the game is not allowed.
  10. Decision of the Judges and Event Organizers shall be treated as final.
  11. AC power supply will be provided at the sight of play for charging purpose only.
  12. Event organiser reserve the right to ask for the explanation of the robot specifications.
  13. The organizers reserve the right to change any or all of the rules as they deem fit. Changes in rules, if any, will be announced on the time of event.
  14. Positions of balls and obstacles in the arena will be decided by the organizers on the particular day of event.

03.Technical Quiz

Rules and regulation

  1. Participants should form a group of minimum two or maximum three students.
  2. Participants must carry a valid identity card of their respective institutions.
  3. The organizers reserve the right to change any or all of the rules as they deem fit. Changes in rules, if any, will be announced on the time of event.
  4. Questions will be asked from the basics of all engineering disciplines, daily life science phenomenon, on the basis of practical applications, current affairs in science and technology.
  5. After Prelims, only 10 top scoring teams will be selected for next rounds.
  6. After Rapid Fire round only 6 teams will be selected for Final round.
  7. 1st, 2nd and 3rd place holders will be awarded after Final round with cash prizes and certificates.
